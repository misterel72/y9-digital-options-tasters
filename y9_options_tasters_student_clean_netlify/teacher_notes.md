# Year 9 Digital Options Tasters - Teacher Notes

## Overview

This pack contains three standalone taster lessons for Year 9 students choosing Year 10 options:

1. BTEC Level 2 IT - Digital Agency Sprint
2. IGCSE ICT - ICT Control Room
3. IGCSE Computer Science - Algorithmic Escape Room

Each lesson is designed for 45 to 60 minutes and includes:

- A teacher-led introduction
- A gamified student mission
- Scoring and feedback
- A final report or certificate that students can screenshot or print
- Accessibility controls
- No login, no database and no personal data collection

## Suggested delivery model

### 45-minute version

- 0 to 10 minutes - Teacher-led intro
- 10 to 35 minutes - Gamified mission
- 35 to 42 minutes - Final report or certificate
- 42 to 45 minutes - Quick reflection: Which subject felt like your kind of challenge?

### 60-minute version

- 0 to 10 minutes - Teacher-led intro
- 10 to 45 minutes - Gamified mission
- 45 to 55 minutes - Pair reflection and final report
- 55 to 60 minutes - Subject identity summary

## Subject identity messages

### BTEC Level 2 IT

You create useful digital products for real clients. This route is applied, practical, creative and evidence-based.

### IGCSE ICT

You use software, data and digital tools accurately to solve real problems. This route is practical, precise and tool-focused.

### IGCSE Computer Science

You understand how computers work and control them using logic and code. This route is problem-solving, algorithmic and technical.

## Netlify deployment

1. Go to Netlify.
2. Create a new site using drag and drop.
3. Upload the whole folder or the zip file.
4. The landing page is `index.html`.
5. Share the Netlify URL with students or turn it into a QR code.

## Files

- `index.html` - landing page for all three tasters
- `btec-digital-agency.html` - BTEC Level 2 IT taster
- `igcse-ict-control-room.html` - IGCSE ICT taster
- `igcse-cs-escape-room.html` - IGCSE Computer Science taster
- `netlify.toml` - basic Netlify static site configuration

## Safeguarding and data note

The activities store only local browser state for scoring. No student data is sent anywhere. Team names are typed into the browser only and can be avoided by asking students to use table numbers or fictional team names.

## Canva teacher-led intro decks

The three lesson pages now include embedded Canva decks in the teacher-led intro section:

- BTEC L2 ICT: Digital Agency Sprint
- IGCSE ICT: ICT Control Room
- IGCSE Computer Science: Algorithmic Escape Room

Each page includes an embedded deck, an "Open Canva deck" link for reliability, and a "Launch mission setup" button to move into the student activity. The embeds use Canva's standard 16:9 iframe format and do not require student logins.

The BTEC Digital Agency Sprint page includes the FutureFest materials students need to complete the client challenge.
