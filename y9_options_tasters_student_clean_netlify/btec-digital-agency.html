BriefingMission stage

Mission setup

Enter your agency details, then complete the sprint challenges. You will generate a final pitch card at the end.


Round 1: Decode the real client requirements

Select the five details that are genuine requirements from the brief. Beware of tempting nonsense. Nonsense often wears a hat and calls itself innovation.

Round 2: Audience fit

Use the persona cards above. Choose the best design decision for each audience need.

Round 3: Product recommendation

Match each client problem to the best digital product. This checks that your choice is purposeful, not just pretty.

Round 4: Build your campaign plan

Use the source pack, personas and product cards to complete your plan. Be specific. Vague answers are where good ideas go to have a nap.


Round 5: Pitch zone

Prepare a 30-second pitch. Use this frame, then generate your evidence card.

Pitch frame: Our team recommends using a [product] because [reason]. This will work for [audience] because [audience need]. Our key message is [slogan/message]. It will encourage people to [action].



Mini glossary

Client brief
The problem, audience, requirements and constraints given by a client.
Audience
The people the product is designed for.
Digital product
A digital outcome such as a poster, form, advert, microsite or social post.
Justification
A clear explanation of why your decision fits the brief.
